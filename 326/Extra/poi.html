Let's show that L⃗:=r⃗×v⃗\vec L : = \vec r\times \vec vL:=r×v is a constant of motion (by brute force computation) if any two components of the vector are constants of motion. We are going to need the following angular momentum components:

Lx=ypz−zpyLy=zpx−xpzLz=xpy−ypx \begin{aligned} L_x &= y p_z - zp_y\\ L_y&= zp_x-xp_z\\ L_z&=xp_y-yp_x \end{aligned} Lx​Ly​Lz​​=ypz​−zpy​=zpx​−xpz​=xpy​−ypx​​

If two components of angular momentum are conserved, the third component and hence the whole vector is conserved. This structure is called the algebra of three dimensional rotations, or, more formally, the special orthogonal Lie algebra of three generators (the LiL_iLi​'s), so(3)\mathfrak{so}(3)so(3). You will learn more about this algebraic (and geometric) structure later.

We have found that the squared magnitude of angular momentum Poisson commutes with the angular momentum. Hence, a system with Hamiltonian

H=L2H=L^2H=L2
has {H,Li}=0\{H,L_i\}=0{H,Li​}=0 and hence conserves angular momentum, as expected.

L2L^2L2 is the quadratic Casimir of the (Lie) algebra of 3 dimensional rotations, so(3)\mathfrak{so}(3)so(3). The Casimir element of a Lie algebra is quadratic in the generators and commutes with every generator.

Planar rotational symmetry

Now let

H=L2+U(q1). H= L^2+U(q_1). H=L2+U(q1​).

The potential that depends on a single component of q⃗\vec qq​ breaks the full rotational symmetry of L2L^2L2. So the only invariant rotations of this system are those that fix q1q_1q1​. Let's prove this.

{U(x),Lx}={U(x),ypz−zpy}={U(x),ypz}−{U(x),zpy}=({U(x),y}pz+{U(r),pz}y)−({U(x),z}py+{U(r),py}z)={U(x),pz}y−{U(x),py}z=−∂zU(x)y−∂yU(x)z=0 \begin{aligned} \{U(x), L_x\} &= \{U(x), y p_z - zp_y\}\\ &= \{U(x), yp_z\} - \{U(x), z p_y\}\\ &= (\{U(x), y\}p_z +\{U(r), p_z\}y) - (\{U(x), z\}p_y+\{U(r), p_y\}z)\\ &=\{U(x), p_z\}y-\{U(x), p_y\}z\\ &= -\partial_z U(x)y - \partial_y U(x)z\\ &= 0 \end{aligned} {U(x),Lx​}​={U(x),ypz​−zpy​}={U(x),ypz​}−{U(x),zpy​}=({U(x),y}pz​+{U(r),pz​}y)−({U(x),z}py​+{U(r),py​}z)={U(x),pz​}y−{U(x),py​}z=−∂z​U(x)y−∂y​U(x)z=0​
